Principles

What I believe.

Accessibility is not optional.

Every product, platform, and website meets WCAG standards. Inclusive design is a technical discipline, not a checkbox exercise.

Ship products, not slide decks.

Working software over extensive documentation. Strategy matters, but execution matters more.

AI should do the repetitive work.

So humans can focus on the creative, strategic, and meaningful work that technology cannot replace.

About

From developer
to CEO.

I started in Portugal, moved to Scotland where I spent a decade as a front-end developer working with brands like Visit Scotland, Vue Cinemas, and Dalmore Distillery, then relocated to Chiang Mai, Thailand, where I have been based for the past ten years.

In Thailand, I spent six years at CLBS as Web Development Manager, collaborating with ebuero in Germany to deliver web and desktop-app projects across international teams. In parallel, I co-founded Landing Pad Digital, a BOI-approved tech startup building AI-powered web platforms.

My path has taken me from writing CSS by hand in 2004 to running a company that ships AI products in 2026. What has not changed is the standard I hold myself to: clean code, accessible interfaces, and technology that serves people.

Today, I lead a remote team, architect cloud infrastructure, build AI tools, and develop the marketing strategies that bring them to market. Equally comfortable in a Docker terminal, a Figma file, or a boardroom.
